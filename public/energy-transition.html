The Energy Transition

Analyzing the shift from fossil fuels to renewable energy and what it means for economies and geopolitics.

← Back to home

About this topic

The world's energy system is undergoing its most significant transformation since the industrial revolution. Solar and wind costs have plummeted, battery technology is advancing rapidly, and the electrification of transport is accelerating.

But the transition isn't simple. Grid stability, mineral supply chains, stranded assets, and political resistance create complex dynamics. This section examines the technology, economics, and politics of moving beyond fossil fuels—and what it means for different countries, industries, and investors.

Key questions I'm exploring

How fast can renewables actually scale? What happens to petrostates as oil demand peaks? Which technologies will win the storage wars? How will the transition reshape global power dynamics, and who stands to gain or lose?
