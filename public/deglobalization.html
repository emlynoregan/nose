Deglobalization

Examining the unwinding of global supply chains and the return of economic nationalism.

About this topic

For decades, the trend was clear: falling trade barriers, lengthening supply chains, and deepening economic interdependence. That era appears to be ending. Trade wars, pandemic disruptions, and great power competition are fragmenting the global economy.

This section explores what deglobalization actually looks like, who's driving it, and what comes next. Is this a temporary disruption or a fundamental shift? What does "friendshoring" and "nearshoring" mean in practice? How will different regions adapt to a more fragmented world economy?

Key questions I'm exploring

How far will deglobalization go? Which industries and countries are most exposed? What are the inflationary implications of shorter, more resilient supply chains? Could we see the emergence of distinct economic blocs, and what would that mean for global development?
